Mutton Curry

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 2 cups water

Procedure

  1. Heat oil in a pressure cooker or pot and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for a minute.
  4. Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the mutton pieces and sauté until they turn brown on all sides.
  7. Whisk the yogurt and add it to the mutton. Stir to coat the meat evenly.
  8. Pour in water and close the lid of the pressure cooker. Cook for 2-3 whistles or for 30 minutes on medium heat in a regular pot, until the mutton is tender and the gravy thickens.
  9. Once done, open the lid, sprinkle garam masala, and mix well.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.
  11. Enjoy with roti, paratha, or rice!
